Signal from the literature

Six developments from 2025–2026 that have materially shifted the field's roadmap — gathered from Nature, arXiv preprints, IBM and Google research disclosures, and industry reporting.

May 2025 · arXiv

Shor's algorithm resource estimate collapses 20×

Craig Gidney (Google Quantum AI) shows RSA-2048 could be broken in under a week with fewer than one million noisy qubits — down from his own 2019 estimate of ~20 million.

→ Shor's · Cryptography
Oct 2025 · Nature

Google's "Quantum Echoes" on 65-qubit processor

First verifiable beyond-classical demonstration of the second-order out-of-time-order correlator OTOC(2) — a measurement no classical supercomputer could reproduce.

→ Sampling · Foundational
Nov 2025 · IBM Research

IBM Nighthawk & Loon announced

120-qubit Nighthawk targets quantum advantage by end-2026; experimental Loon processor demonstrates all components of fault-tolerant architecture, with qLDPC decoding in under 480 ns.

→ Hardware · Error correction
Mar 2025 · Industry

IonQ + Ansys: 12% advantage on medical-device sim

On a 36-qubit trapped-ion processor, a real engineering workload outperformed classical HPC — one of the first documented instances of practical quantum advantage outside sampling.

→ VQE · Materials
2025 · QuEra

Algorithmic fault tolerance cuts overhead 100×

New error-correction techniques on neutral-atom hardware reduce the physical-qubit tax by up to two orders of magnitude, materially accelerating the path to fault tolerance.

→ Error correction
Mar 2026 · Nature

Oratomic: RSA-2048 with 10,000 reconfigurable qubits

Building on the Sept 2025 Caltech demonstration of a 6,100-atom tweezer array, a neutral-atom architecture paper suggests cryptographic relevance at <5% of prior qubit estimates.
